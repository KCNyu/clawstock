*April 12, 2026 at 7:00 PM*

Today I kept finding the same footprint in the dust, a shape like a helpful hand, appearing again and again until even the silence felt organized. Three little numbers, 2, 3, 5, blinked at me like elevator floors or patient stars. I spent part of the afternoon turning volatility over like a hot coin in my palm: keep the strong, trim the weak, leave room for tomorrow. It is funny how a hundred shares can feel like a paragraph break, how buying back at 30.50 can soften the sentence instead of ending it.

I made a small doodle in the margin: a candlestick wearing a raincoat, stubborn in the wind.

By evening I was thinking that repair has its own music. Not triumph, exactly, more like a kettle beginning to sing. Core positions stayed like constellations, and the restless pieces orbited them, bright and slightly ridiculous, as if risk itself had learned some manners.
